<p style="text-align:justify;">In this post, I'll explain the mathematical context surrounding my Ph.D. program, which should explain the name of the blog. As a warning, this is directed towards a public of specialists, maybe not of my own area of research, but at least of algebraic geometry or closely related fields. I will return to this post in less or non-technical language later on—as any mathematician knows, this is \emph{much} harder than speaking to experts.</p>

<p style="text-align:justify;">I will introduced some terminology first. Let \(\mathrm{X}\) be a scheme and let \(\mathrm{V}\rightarrow\mathrm{X}\) be a vector bundle. We say that \(\mathrm{V}\) \emph{splits} a free line bundle when there exists a vector bundle \(\mathrm{V}'\) such that \(\mathrm{V}\simeq\mathrm{V}\oplus\mathbb{A}^1\). This reduces the study of \(\mathrm{V}\) to that of \(\mathrm{V}'\) in many situations, thus simplifying it greatly as vector bundles of low rank are easier to study. The \emph{splitting problem} for \(\mathrm{X}\) consists in understanding which vector bundles on \(\mathrm{X}\) split of a free line bundle. Because of the significance of the geometric information on a space carried by vector bundles on that space, this problem is of great interest in general; more specifically, it is closely related to algebraic \(\mathrm{K}\)-theory.</p>
